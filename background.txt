
In the year 2013, HitHub, a popular social coding platform has
successfully created a new technology that allows a Git repository
within a Git repository.

Developers would think that they are coding on the Master branch in
their own private repository while the HitHub staff will infiltrate
their Git repository for corporate espionage.

This has caused severe destruction of many companies when their products
were suddenly pre-empted by HitHub. To make matters worse, their own
code was missing - effectively rendering any form of litigation useless.

That is until 2020. When an unnamed employee from the now defunct
company HitBucket decides to take down HitHub once and for all - using
their own technology against themselves...
